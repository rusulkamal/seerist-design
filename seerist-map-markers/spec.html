Map Markers

Every marker on the Seerist map answers two questions instantly — what kind of thing is this and how much should I trust it — without the user needing to read anything.

01 Visual Language

Each visual property of a marker carries exactly one meaning. Nothing is decorative.

Shape → Content type
Circle = Event. Rounded square = Analysis or News & Social. Filled circle = Asset.
Border → Event status
Solid = Verified. Dashed = Unverified. Dashed + clock badge = Future. Dashed + spark badge = Breaking.
Colour → Severity
Red = high severity. Orange = medium. Grey = low.
Icon → Category
The icon inside the marker shows the type category (terrorism, maritime, unrest, etc.).

03 Event Markers

Events are incidents on the ground, curated and verified by Seerist. They are the most authoritative markers on the map.

Seerist-produced. Events are created by Seerist analysts and editorial processes — not ingested directly from third-party feeds. This makes them the highest-trust content type on the map.
Shape & Size

Circle, 34 × 34 px. A circle communicates a point in space where something physically happened. White background so severity colour reads clearly against any map style.

04 News & Social Markers

Third-party reporting — news articles, wire feeds, and social media posts about a topic in an area. Less authoritative than Seerist Events.

Third-party source. News & Social markers have not been independently verified by Seerist analysts. They provide signal and context but should be treated with appropriate caution.
Shape & Style
Rounded square (squircle), 34 × 34 px, white background, slate border (#94a3b8).
The squircle shape is the instant signal: this is not a Seerist ground-truth event.
ShapeRounded square — border-radius 8px
Border colourSlate #94a3b8
IconMatches the event category (same icons as events — bullhorn, anchor, etc.)
Visible fromZoom level 2 and above
How to read it alongside events

A bullhorn circle next to a bullhorn squircle means: Seerist has a verified protest event here, and the media is also reporting on protests in this area. Same icon, different shape — source is immediately obvious without reading a label.

05 Analysis Markers

In-depth written assessments produced by Seerist Analysts about a region or topic.

Seerist-produced. Analysis is authored by Seerist's analyst team — not third-party. The green border distinguishes it from News & Social (slate border) and communicates high trust.
Shape & Style
Rounded square (squircle), same shape as News & Social — both are intel content rather than ground events.
Green border and icon = Seerist-authored, high trust.
ShapeRounded square — border-radius 8px
Border colourGreen #10b981
Icon colourMatches the green border
IconDocument / file-lines — represents a written report
Visible fromZoom level 2 and above

06 Asset Markers

The user's own tracked locations — offices, field teams, embassies, vessels, facilities. Completely separate from events and intel.

Shape & Style
Solid coloured fill — immediately distinct from all other marker types. Each asset type has its own colour.
ShapeCircle, 32 × 32 px
FillSolid colour (no white background) — instantly recognisable as an asset
ClusteringNever clustered — always visible at all zoom levels
FilteringToggled via the Assets layer in the Layers panel (independent of event filters)

08 Same-Location Stack

When two or more non-breaking events share the exact same coordinates, they are shown as a stacked group rather than overlapping circles.

3
Three stacked rings with the event count on the front. Clicking opens a list in the popover where users can select any individual event from the group.
Breaking eventsAlways shown as individual markers — the stack count only reflects non-breaking events
Click actionOpens a stack list view in the popover — user selects an event to see its full detail

10 Interactions

What happens when users interact with markers.

ActionResult
Click event markerOpens detail popover — title, location, time, source, category, and action buttons (Open article, Save, Share)
Click stack markerOpens stack list view — all events at that location, tap any to see full detail
Hover analysis / news markerShows tooltip with the document or article title
Click clusterPlanned: zooms in to expand the cluster into individual markers
Click map backgroundCloses any open popover and deselects the active marker
