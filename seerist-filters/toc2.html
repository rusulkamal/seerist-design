Overview
Published May 18, 2026

Political risk will remain elevated over the coming years following the Taliban takeover in August 2021. Several government ministers are members of the Haqqani Network, which has strong ties to al-Qaida.

The economy remains isolated, and the humanitarian crisis has worsened amid a suspension of access to financial lines and international aid. Companies and foreign organisations will face heightened exposure to sanctions and reputational risk.

Political
Published May 18, 2026

The Taliban's Supreme Leader Haibatullah Akhundzada remains the ultimate authority, ruling by decree from Kandahar. The movement remains deeply factionalised.

Any prospect of international recognition remains remote. The Taliban's refusal to form an inclusive government has foreclosed engagement with most states.

Operational
Published May 18, 2026

Operating in Afghanistan requires navigating a complex regulatory environment. Unofficial payments to local commanders are common and create significant compliance exposure.

Supply chains remain severely disrupted. The banking sector remains largely non-functional for international transfers.

Security
Published May 18, 2026

Islamic State Khorasan Province (ISKP) remains the principal terrorist threat, conducting high-casualty attacks in Kabul.

Kidnapping for ransom remains a serious risk for foreign nationals and NGO workers.

Cyber
Published May 18, 2026

Afghanistan's cyber threat environment is primarily shaped by state-sponsored actors from neighbouring countries.

Organisations operating in-country should assume network traffic is subject to interception.
